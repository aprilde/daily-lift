# Discovery (brief)

A short note on the discovery that preceded the prototype. Kept deliberately light - this repo's focus is the AI-assisted execution, not the discovery, which is standard PM work.

## Who it's for

Beginners working out at home - specifically women new to exercise who feel out of place in typical fitness apps and aren't sure what the exercises are or how to do them safely.

## The core need

A simple weekly routine that stays **visible and top of mind** without relying on push notifications, paired with **plain-language guidance** so each exercise is understandable to someone doing it for the first time.

## Key insights that shaped the product

- A glanceable home screen presence (a widget) removes the friction of "remembering to open an app" - the routine is just *there*.
- For true beginners, exercise names alone are meaningless; form guidance is essential, not a nice-to-have.
- Intimidation is a real barrier - the tone and visuals need to feel encouraging, not gym-oriented.
- Weights and reps change week to week, so the routine has to be easily editable by the user.

## What discovery ruled out

Prebuilt workout apps were considered and set aside: none combined a day-based glanceable widget with genuinely beginner-friendly, unintimidating guidance for the at-home audience.

These insights fed directly into the prototype in `1-prototype/`.
